Pancreatic cancer doesn’t have to remain one of the world’s deadliest cancers. Together, we can keep more people from becoming a statistic. By supporting research for early detection and innovative treatments, you can help us change the future and save the lives of millions.

5 Things Everyone Should Know

Despite being one of the most deadly forms of cancer, pancreatic cancer receives less than 2% of overall cancer research funding.

Symptoms of Pancreatic Cancer

The early symptoms of pancreatic cancer are often vague or unnoticeable. It is only later, when more specific apparent symptoms arise, that patients and doctors consider pancreatic cancer a possibility. By that time, it is often too late for surgery that could have potentially cured the cancer.

Not everyone will have all these symptoms. For example, those who have a tumour in the body or tail of the pancreas are unlikely to have jaundice.

All of these symptoms can have other causes, and there is not yet a reliable and easy diagnostic test for pancreatic cancer.

If you regularly experience ONE OR MORE of these symptoms which are NOT NORMAL FOR YOU, DO NOT IGNORE THEM, contact your doctor right away.

Get More Info

Classic pancreatic cancer symptoms can include:

Jaundice (yellow skin/eyes, dark urine). This is related to a bile duct obstruction.

Weight loss which is significant and unexplained.

Abdominal pain which is new-onset and significant.

Other possible symptoms of pancreatic cancer include:

Pain in the upper abdomen that radiates to the back which is new and significant.

Pain in the mid-back.

Diabetes which is new-onset in people over the age of 50 who do not have risk factors for the disease.

Vague indigestion or abdominal discomfort.

Loss of appetite.

Nausea and vomiting.

Pain when eating.

Fatty stools that are often pale and smell foul.
